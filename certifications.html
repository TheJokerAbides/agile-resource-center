Agile Certifications Guide

A practical breakdown of the most recognised agile certifications — what they cover, who they're for, and the honest pros and cons of each. Use this to decide what's worth your time and money.

PSM I
Professional Scrum Master I
Scrum.org · PSM I
Scrum Beginner friendly
⌄
Cost
~$200 USD
Exam format
80 questions, 60 min
Pass mark
85%
Difficulty
Moderate
Renewal
Never expires
Prerequisites
None
✓ Pros
  • No mandatory training — study at your own pace
  • Relatively affordable at ~$200
  • Never expires — no renewal fees
  • Highly respected and widely recognised
  • Rigorous exam tests real understanding, not memorisation
✕ Cons
  • High pass mark (85%) — requires thorough preparation
  • No classroom component included
  • Less name recognition outside tech than CSM
  • Free retake not included
Best for: Self-motivated learners who want a rigorous, cost-effective Scrum certification that never needs renewing. A great first cert for developers and aspiring Scrum Masters.
CSM
Certified ScrumMaster
Scrum Alliance · CSM
Scrum Beginner friendly
⌄
Cost
$1,000–$1,500+
Exam format
50 questions, 60 min
Pass mark
74%
Difficulty
Accessible
Renewal
Every 2 years
Prerequisites
2-day training required
✓ Pros
  • 2-day instructor-led training included in cost
  • Most widely recognised cert among non-technical hiring managers
  • Strong Scrum Alliance community and network
  • Lower pass mark makes it more accessible
  • Good for career changers entering agile roles
✕ Cons
  • Significantly more expensive than PSM I
  • Renewal fees every 2 years (SEUs required)
  • Mandatory training can feel like box-ticking
  • Exam considered less rigorous than PSM I
  • Cost varies widely by training provider
Best for: Those who benefit from structured classroom learning and want the most broadly recognised Scrum brand, especially when targeting non-tech organisations or enterprise roles.

PSM I recommended
✓ Pros
  • Demonstrates genuine advanced Scrum mastery
  • No expiry — permanent credential
  • Great differentiator for experienced Scrum Masters
  • Still affordable relative to content depth
✕ Cons
  • Significantly harder than PSM I — requires real experience
  • Less recognised than PSM I at entry level
  • Scenario-based questions require deep situational judgement
Best for: Experienced Scrum Masters with 2+ years of practice who want to prove advanced competency and differentiate themselves from the sea of PSM I / CSM holders.

No exam — attendance only
Pass mark
N/A
Difficulty
Accessible
Renewal
Every 2 years
Prerequisites
2-day training required
✓ Pros
  • No exam required — just attend the training
  • Good foundation for Product Owners new to Scrum
  • Widely requested in job descriptions
  • Strong focus on stakeholder collaboration
✕ Cons
  • No exam means it doesn't truly validate knowledge
  • Expensive for what is essentially a 2-day course certificate
  • Quality varies massively by training provider
  • Renewal costs and SEU requirements add up
Best for: Product Owners or BAs who need a recognised credential quickly and whose employer will fund the training. The lack of an exam means it's easy to obtain but less meaningful as a signal of expertise.

SAFe Agilist (SA)
Scaled Agile Inc · SA / Leading SAFe
Scaled Agile Intermediate
⌄
Cost
$995–$1,295+
Exam format
45 questions, 90 min
Pass mark
73%
Difficulty
Moderate
Renewal
Every year
Prerequisites
2-day training required
✓ Pros
  • Dominant framework in large enterprise organisations
  • High demand in financial services, government and large tech
  • Covers PI Planning, ARTs, and enterprise-level agility
  • Well-structured learning path with multiple role-based certs
✕ Cons
  • Annual renewal fee ($100+) even after passing
  • Controversial — critics argue SAFe dilutes agile values
  • Heavy and complex framework not suited to all organisations
  • Expensive relative to depth of individual learning
  • Less useful outside large enterprise contexts
Best for: Agile practitioners working in or targeting large enterprises that have adopted SAFe. Less relevant for small to medium teams or organisations focused on pure Scrum or Kanban.

Prerequisites
KMP I (Team Kanban)
✓ Pros
  • Deep focus on flow, WIP limits and evolutionary change
  • No expiry once completed
  • Highly practical — applicable immediately
  • Well respected in software and operations industries
✕ Cons
  • Less widely recognised than Scrum certs in job ads
  • Requires two separate courses to achieve KMP
  • Smaller community compared to Scrum Alliance
Best for: Operations managers, team leads and agile coaches who want to apply Kanban at a systemic level. Excellent complement to Scrum certifications for well-rounded practitioners.
PMI-ACP
PMI Agile Certified Practitioner
Project Management Institute · PMI-ACP
Multi-framework Advanced
⌄
Cost
$435–$495 USD
Exam format
120 questions, 3 hours
Pass mark
Not published
Difficulty
Challenging
Renewal
Every 3 years (PDUs)
Prerequisites
2,000 hrs agile exp.
✓ Pros
  • Covers multiple frameworks — Scrum, Kanban, XP, Lean
  • Highly regarded in project management circles
  • Strong signal of broad agile experience
  • PMI brand is globally recognised
  • Good for PMs transitioning to agile delivery
✕ Cons
  • Requires 2,000 hours of agile project experience
  • PDU maintenance every 3 years
  • Broad coverage means less depth in any one framework
  • Less recognised than CSM/PSM for pure Scrum Master roles
Best for: Experienced project managers and delivery leads with existing PMI credentials who want to formally demonstrate agile breadth. Less suitable as a first certification.
